I have the impression that it was clear that XFree86 was getting bogged down, that it was difficult and tiring to contribute, and there was no 'leadership' of the project. The 'core' group, argued that the project was just code written by individuals, so no leadership was possible or necessary.   Discussions were getting bogged down and it difficult to work out the scope of the project because there didn't appear to be anyone to give a definitive answer.  The 'core' group seemed to believe that the problems were because the code was difficult to write and therefore it was difficult to find contributors, the persistent refrain was 'no one is stopping you doing what you want so just shut up and write code'.  

The reason the project did get forked was because a critical mass of people began to believe that this non-leadership leadership was making it difficult and unpleasant to get work done, therefore repelling contributors and stalling the project.   The lack of leadership made it hard to have any long term strategy. 

Numpy also has these themes:  'show me the code', 'we don't need leadership but we do need code', 'there's no leadership problem'.  It is difficult, boring and unpleasant to discuss major or even minor code changes, let alone structural changes.
